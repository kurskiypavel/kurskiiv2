An Energy Crisis

The Christmas tree is a tradition associated with the celebration of Christmas. It is normally decorated with Christmas lights and colorful ornaments during the days around Christmas.

December 28, 1972. The New York Stock Exchange tree lighting tradition started in 1923. The lighting ceremony is one of the oldest in New York City and one of the most visited city attractions during the holidays.

National Archives & Records Administration

On November 7, 1973, President Nixon, in a nationwide television address, spoke about the origins of the energy crisis and offered a comprehensive solution which included a nationwide 55-mile-per-hour speed limit, increased use of carpools, turning down thermostats to below 70 degrees Fahrenheit, a reduction in commercial aviation flights, and pulling the plug on outdoor Christmas lighting. These policies were presented as “Project Independence,” an overall effort to help reduce the United States’ dependence on overseas fuel.
