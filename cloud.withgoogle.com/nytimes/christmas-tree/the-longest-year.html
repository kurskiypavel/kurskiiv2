Published: December 28, 1972 Copyright © The New York Times

A leap second is a one-second adjustment occasionally applied to the world’s timekeeping system to make up for the Earth’s slowing rotation. Inserting an occasional second is required to keep the Earth’s clocks in time with the apparent rotation of the stars.

Because 1972 was also a leap year (an extra day added at the end of February), the combination of the additional two seconds and day made it the longest year-to-date in the history of recorded time.
