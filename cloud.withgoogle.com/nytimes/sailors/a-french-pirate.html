A French Pirate

The Hudson River is a 315-mile river that flows from north to south primarily through eastern New York in the United States. It is navigable by large ships as far north as Troy, New York and by ocean-faring vessels up to the Port of Albany.

The Jean Bart had a very short career in service. It was commissioned in 1949 and decommissioned in 1961, then scrapped in 1969.

DBPedia

The battleship is named after Jean Bart (1650-1702), a French privateer (pirate) known for his naval skill. In his hometown of Dunkirk, France, locals call themselves the “children of Jean Bart” and sing songs about him at the town’s yearly carnival festival.

DBPedia + WWII CATALOG

"Carnival Dunkerque" by Patrick Vandenbussche, retouched by Derek Jensen, Wikimedia CC BY-SA 3.0

The Dunkirk Carnival is one of the most exuberant carnivals (a festive season before the liturgical season of Lent) in Europe. Its traditions date to the 17th century and are based on the vischerbende where fishermen went from one cafe to another, accompanied by their relatives and friends, just before departing to fishing grounds in Iceland.
