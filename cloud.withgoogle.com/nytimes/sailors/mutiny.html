A Mutiny

The Hudson River runs through eastern New York in the United States. Sections of the river reach a maximum depth exceeding 200 feet.

The Hudson River is named after the English sea explorer and navigator, Henry Hudson, who was best known for his extensive explorations of present-day Canada and the northeastern United States.

Google Books

In the spring of 1611, Hudson planned to further explore Hudson Bay in hopes of finding the Northeast Passage to China on behalf of the Dutch East India Company.

After wintering on the shore of James Bay, Hudson wanted to push west to find the passage through the Arctic Circle. The crew mutinied and in June put Hudson, his son, and seven crewmen on a small open boat, marooning them in Hudson Bay. They were never seen again.
