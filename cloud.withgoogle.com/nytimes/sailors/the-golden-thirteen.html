In 1941, President Franklin D. Roosevelt signed executive order 8802 that prohibited racial discrimination by government agencies. The Navy soon began a two-month officer training course which commissioned 13 African American men who came to be known as the “Golden Thirteen.”

Federal Register API

These thirteen men helped to change the very face of American military. In the United States Navy today are more than three thousand African-American officers who walked the door that the Golden Thirteen opened in 1944.

USN Demographic

In April 1982 , the Navy brought the eight surviving members of The Golden Thirteen back to sea for a reunion. They were flown 20 miles out from the Norfolk Naval Air Station to stay for three days on the Kidd, a new guided missile destroyer.
