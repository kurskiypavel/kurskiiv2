Two years after the top photo was taken, the International Building was built on the lot across the street. In the courtyard of this building stands “Atlas,” a 15-foot tall bronze statue of the Greek Titan holding the sky (not the Earth) aloft.

Google Books + DBPedia

In Greek mythology, Atlas instructed humans in the art of astrology, which helped sailors to navigate and farmers to measure the seasons. Atlas led the Titans in a war against Zeus and the gods of Olympus. After the Titans’ defeat, Zeus punished Atlas by making him carry the heavens for eternity.

423 feet away from “Atlas,” in the middle of Rockefeller Center, sits Atlas’ brother “Prometheus” — an 18-foot tall, 8-ton gilded cast-bronze sculpture. Like his brother, Prometheus helped humankind and represents sacrifice and striving despite a great cost.
