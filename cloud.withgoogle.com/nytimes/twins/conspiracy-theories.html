Conspiracy Theories

Cast-iron utility hole covers stamped with the letters G.P.O. provide access to the many General Post Office subsurface cable chambers that house miles of telephone wires underneath the streets of London.

DBPedia

In the 1940s and 50s, the GPO began secretly constructing a network of deep level tunnels — accessible via certain cable chambers — that would protect the government in case of nuclear war.

Google Books

"Tunnels" via Duncan Campbell, © Chris Davies

In 1980 a journalist, Duncan Campbell, gained entry into a section of the secret tunnels where he photographed and mapped his findings. The Post Office denied his claims saying he staged and shot it in a studio.

Google Books

“Campbell rode around the tunnels for hours covering in excess of a dozen miles in total, but it was clear to him that he had barely scratched the surface of London’s extraordinary underground tunnel work.”

Google Books

Who Owns England

In November 2017, the first official confirmation of the existence of deep level tunnels occurred with the public release of data from Her Majesty’s Land Registry.

HM Land Registry Dataset

"Q1 building at The National Archives", The National Archives (UK), Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported

The full extent of the work done on the tunnel system is currently locked in the National Archives for 75 years. The files will become declassified in April 2026.

Google Books

Recap

21 years after the photo was taken, journalist Duncan Campbell exposed the existence of a secret government tunnel system accessible via certain GPO utility hole covers. The full extent of the tunnels remain classified until 2026.
