A Populist Rebellion

St. Magnus the Martyr is a Church of England church and parish located at Lower Thames Street near The Monument to the Great Fire of London.

DBPedia

Public Domain

Running perpendicular to Lower Thames Street is Fish Street Hill, where the women are walking. It was once a bustling thoroughfare and the main connection to Old London Bridge, the city’s key crossing point over the River Thames for 600 years.

In Shakespeare’s “Henry VI, Part 2,” Jack Cade, the rebel leader of an uprising against the government of Henry VI, leads his forces across the bridge and shouts, “Up Fish Street! Down St. Magnus’ corner! Kill and knock down! Throw them into Thames!”

The rebellion against Henry VI was the largest populist uprising to take place in England during the 15th century. It culminated in a bloody battle on London Bridge when the citizens of London turned on the rebels.

Recap

Fish Street Hill, the same street on which the women are walking, was once a main connection to Old London Bridge. In 1450, rebel leader Jack Cade marched along the same route during the largest populist uprising in 15th century England.
