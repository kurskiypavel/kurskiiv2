Felix the Cat

The Mayflower Hotel sat across from Central Park at 15 Central Park West. An understated 18-story, 600-room building, the Mayflower was designed by Emery Roth in 1926.

Google Maps Platform + DBpedia

"The Mayflower. 15 Central Park West", Wurts Bros., via MCNY

In “Mansions in the Clouds: The Skyscraper Palazzi of Emery Roth” (Balsam Press, 1986), Steven Ruttenbaum says of the Mayflower: “This structure was yet another of Roth’s fine background buildings” and “made a valuable contribution to the New York streetscape.”

Google Books

One of the notable residents of the Mayflower was Patrick Sullivan, an Australian-American cartoonist, pioneer animator, and film producer best known for producing the first Felix the Cat silent cartoons.

DBpedia

U.S. National Archives

Felix is an anthropomorphic cat created in the silent film era and the first animated character to become popular enough to draw movie audiences. His screen debut came on November 9, 1919 in an animated short film called “Feline Follies.”

Internet Archive Search

"1927-1928 Felix the Cat Years", Macy's via Wikia

Felix was also the first cartoon character balloon to fly in the Macy’s parade, in 1931. In that year, Felix was full of oxygen, not helium which came much later. In 2016, after 85 years away, Felix made his triumphant comeback in the parade.
