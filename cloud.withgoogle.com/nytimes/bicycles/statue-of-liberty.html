The Statue of Liberty

At the time of the photo, a Byrrh establishment was located at 33 Avenue Des Champs-Élysées.

Google Maps Platform

Created in 1866 by brothers Pallade and Simon Violet, Byrrh is a french apértif made of red wine, alcohol, grape juice, and quinine.

Google Books

Byrrh’s production factory is located in the southern French town of Thuir. The Violet family constructed the factory and hired the civil engineer Gustave Eiffel to design a vast station hall to receive shipment trains.

Dbpedia

Six years earlier, Eiffel completed his most important American work, the Statue Of Liberty. The copper neoclassical sculpture was a gift from the people of France and commemorates the alliance between the two countries during the American Revolution.

Google Maps Platform + Dbpedia

The statue quickly became a symbol of freedom, an important tourist destination, and a welcoming sight for immigrants arriving from abroad.

Google Books

Recap

Gustave Eiffel, the structural engineer responsible for the Byrhh factory’s station hall, also designed the interior supporting structure of the Statue of Liberty – a symbol of freedom in America.
