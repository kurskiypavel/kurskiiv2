Ramesses II is often regarded as the greatest, most celebrated and powerful pharaoh of the New Kingdom. His successors and later Egyptians called him the “Great Ancestor.”

In 1974, 21 years after this photo was taken, Ramesses II was issued a valid Egyptian passport so that his 3,000-year-old mummy could be flown to Paris for necessary repair at the Paris Ethnological Museum, .7 miles from the Champs-Élysées.

When the plane with Ramesses’ remains arrived in Paris, the mummy was greeted by a procession and received military honors.

Recap

At the end of Champs-Élysées stands an ancient Egyptian obelisk decorated with hieroglyphs exalting the reign of the king Ramesses II. That same Ramesses II was issued a valid passport so that his 3,000-year-old mummy could be flown to Paris for repair — the first pharaoh in history to hold an official Egyptian passport.
