The Avenue des Champs-Élysées is a 1.2 mile long and 230-foot wide avenue in the 8th arrondissement of Paris, France.

The week before the photo was taken, the ballet “The Rite Of Spring” celebrated its 40th anniversary. The original premiere took place at Paris’s Théâtre des Champs-Élysées, 0.7 miles away from where the above photo was taken.

“The Rite of Spring” is a ballet and orchestral concert work choreographed by Vaslav Najinsky and scored by Igor Stravinsky. The music contains avant-garde experiments in tonality, meter, rhythm, stress, and dissonance.

Google Books + DBpedia

On the night of the premiere, the ballet’s music and choreography shocked many concertgoers and provoked a notorious riot in the audience that eventually grew so loud the music could not be heard.

NY Times Article Search Api

Discarding conventional harmonies for bizarrely dissonant chords, and uniform metrics for harshly jarring beat patterns, he created a sensational theater piece that caused the music world’s most talked about riot.

Recap

“The Rite of Spring” was performed at the Théâtre des Champs-Élysées, less than a mile away from this photo. The ballet was so forward-thinking and controversial it caused one of the most famous riots in the history of the performing arts.
