Manhattan is the most densely populated of New York City’s five boroughs. The island’s iconic sites include skyscrapers such as the Empire State Building, neon-lit Times Square, and Central Park.

July 20, 1969. The crowd in the photo is gathered at Central Park’s Sheep Meadow to watch the Apollo 11 Moon landing. On the same day of this photo, the Newport Folk Festival occured.

DBPedia

150,000 people attended the Newport Folk festival, headlined by Joni Mitchell, Johnny Cash, and James Taylor.

DBPedia

When U.S. astronauts landed on the Moon, the organizers stopped the show to share the broadcast. Folk Festival promoter George Wein came out on stage and announced that the astronauts had landed on the Moon. “Now top that,” he said. “The concert’s over.”

The Newport Folk and Jazz festivals influenced many other concerts, in particular, the famous festival that was held two weeks later, Woodstock

Google Books

The original Woodstock music festival was held on a dairy farm in the Catskill Mountains, northwest of New York City, between August 15–18, 1969. The concert attracted an audience of more than 400,000 people.

Recap

While Neil Armstrong walked on the moon, the Newport Folk Festival was abruptly cut short. Two weeks later, Woodstock, the festival that defined the spirit of a generation, was held in the Catskill Mountains in upstate New York.

Barry Z. Levine
