Central Park is an urban park in Manhattan, New York City, roughly bounded by Fifth Avenue on the east, Eighth Avenue on the west, 59th Street on the south, and 110th Street on the north.

DBPedia

Central Park was established in 1857 on 778 acres of land acquired by the city.

In 1858, landscape architect Frederick Law Olmsted and architect/landscape designer Calvert Vaux won a design competition to improve and expand the park with a plan they titled the “Greensward Plan.”

DBPedia

Public Domain

Frederick Law Olmsted was also an early and important activist in the nature conservation movement that included protecting America's first state park, Niagara Falls. He believed that parks should be places of natural beauty where “the masses could be renewed.”

The New York Public Library

Before being protected, private interests largely controlled the lands surrounding Niagara Falls and public access to the falls was limited. Olmsted applied his philosophy of general use for everyone and created an entire network of footpaths through wooded areas and along the banks of the Niagara River.

Recap

Frederick Law Olmsted, the landscape architect of Central park, is rightly considered to be the father of American landscape architecture. He was a key figure in the nation’s most significant early examples of scenic preservation including America’s first state park, Niagara Falls.
